Overview

Based on research on the existing product, its customers, other products from competitors, and mortgage rules and regulations, I suggested two different approaches. Updating visuals with minimal changes and overhauling the application. The stakeholders wanted to go with a fast way, updating visuals.

Even though it was a visual update, I believed there would be some room and opportunities to make general UX better. I threw a presentation about some opportunities that can improve the product while updating visuals.

Funnel type of data entry

I have been exposed enough to understand several regulations regarding the mortgage application and some marketing techniques. Combining these two into one, I suggested gathering estimated information is better than capturing the precise information upfront.

TRID rule requires lenders to provide loan estimates and closing disclosures to the borrower within 3 business days. The timer starts clocking once the borrower provides certain information. However, lenders easily guess some values of that information.

Instead of doing that, I suggested separate data entry into two parts, assigning easy and attracting ones up-front and complicated and time consuming ones later, in another existing product, borrower portal.
