Mortgage Application

UX Design, UI Design, Research

2023

 
Overview

Based on researches on the existing product, its customers, other products from competitors, and mortgage rules and regulations, I suggested two different approaches. Updating visual with minimal changes and overhaul the application. The stakeholders wanted to go with fast way, updating visuals.

Even though it’s a visual update, I believed there would be some room and opportunities to make general UX better. I threw a presentation about some opportunities that can improve the product while updating visuals.

 
Information Hierarchy

Even though the originating system that was connected to the product has certain items in a same group, the product didn’t need to follow the same grouping rule. There was a consumer report including review of this product which states navigating issue.

I broke down the Uniform Residential Loan Application, URLA, into several parts along with other competitors’ and ours. The flow chart was shared to any new project contributors for an easy and quick overview.

 
Funnel type of data entry

I have been exposed enough to understand several regulations about the mortgage application and some marketing techniques. Combining these two into one, I suggested gathering estimated information is better than capture the precise information upfront.

TRID rule requires lenders to provide loan estimate and closing disclosures to the borrower within 3 business days. The timer starts clocking once the borrower provides certain information. However, lenders easily guess some values of those information.

The existing product asked all information at once as the product meant to work by itself, fully automated mortgage process. However, the prior internal research conducted by third party company suggested that it’s not worth it.

Instead of doing that, I suggested separate data entry into two parts, assigning easy and attracting ones up-front and complicated and time consuming ones later, in another existing product, borrower portal.

 
Data-specific controls

The other product of the company, proprietary system for employees had already achieved very successful data-specific controls. However, customer facing ones didn’t get chances to have those. VP of engineering also agreed that the function could work as it’s not connected to any backend codes.

 
Prototyping

Stakeholders agreed on this and I started making a full design that has a full prototype. This prototype could branch off based on prototype user selection seamlessly. Thanks to the new variable update from Figma.
